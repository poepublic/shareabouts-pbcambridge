{{#_}}Participatory Budgeting (PB) is a democratic process that empowers community members to decide how to spend part of a public budget. In Cambridge the intention is for PB is to directly involve residents in the budgeting and city-building process, foster civic engagement and community spirit, and help ensure that the City's budget reflects the priorities of Cambridge residents and local partners.{{/_}}

{{#_}}The City of Cambridge launched the 10th Cycle of PB and collected ideas from the community between September 11 – October 9, 2023, about how to spend $2 million on projects to improve Cambridge. On the map, you can find all the submitted ideas for PB10. From October 2023 through January 2024, volunteer PB Delegates will research all submitted project ideas and develop the final proposals for the PB ballot. From March 7-17, 2024, all Cambridge residents age 12 and older, regardless of citizenship status, will be eligible to vote for their favorite projects and decide how to spend $2 million of the City's Budget.{{/_}}
