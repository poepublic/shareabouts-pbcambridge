{{#_}}Participatory Budgeting (PB) in Cambridge{{/_}}

{{#_}}PB is a democratic process that gives Cambridge community members the power to decide how to spend $1,000,000 of the City's FY26 Budget.{{/_}}

{{#can_add_places}}

{{#_}}If you have an idea for a project to improve Cambridge, please share it on the map!{{/_}} {{#_}}Explore ideas from others in the community using the “Show Ideas as a List” tab on the upper right of the page or directly through the map.{{/_}}

{{#_}}Winning projects from past PB cycles include:{{/_}}

  • {{#_}}STEAM Upgrades for Youth Centers{{/_}}
  • {{#_}}Home Essentials for Newly Housed Residents{{/_}}
  • {{#_}}Shaded seating areas on hot streets{{/_}}
  • {{#_}}Pollinator gardens{{/_}}
  • {{#_}}And many others!{{/_}}
{{^}}

{{#_}}The City of Cambridge launched the 11th Cycle of PB and collected ideas from the community about how to spend $1 million on projects to improve Cambridge. On the map, you can find all the submitted ideas for PB11. From October 2024 through January 2025, volunteer PB Delegates will research all submitted project ideas and develop the final proposals for the PB ballot. In March, 2025, all Cambridge residents age 12 and older, regardless of citizenship status, will be eligible to vote for their favorite projects and decide how to spend $1 million of the City's Budget.{{/_}}
